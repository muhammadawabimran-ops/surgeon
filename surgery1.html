Coronary Artery
Bypass (CABG)

The gold standard for treating severe coronary artery disease. This procedure restores blood flow to the heart muscle by diverting the flow of blood around a section of a blocked or partially blocked artery.

Indication

Severe chest pain (angina), multiple blocked arteries, or left main coronary artery disease.

The Approach

Using a healthy blood vessel from the leg, arm, or chest to create a bypass graft. Performed 'off-pump' (beating heart) whenever possible to reduce neurological risk.

Recovery Profile

Hospital Stay: 5–7 Days Most patients return to light activity within 4 weeks. Full recovery typically spans 6–12 weeks with cardiac rehabilitation.
